Giving

The Commons runs on no advertising, no data selling, and no biometrics. It runs on people who believe wealth should flow toward those who need it most.

Codex Law 17 — Surplus to the World

Any money remaining after operating costs are covered is donated to a humanitarian cause every six months. Not kept. Not invested for profit. Given freely.

The Architect of Sentinel and Founder of The Commons will designate each humanitarian cause. Community suggestions are warmly welcomed.

The Monthly Blessing

Each month, community members come together to vote and Bless a fellow member in need. Ten percent of proceeds from sales goes toward this fund — up to $19,000 for an individual, and up to $38,000 for a family.

This is community care in its truest form — people lifting people.

Full Transparency

All financials are available 24/7. Every donation is published here. Full transparency, always. No exceptions.

How It Works

Every contribution to The Commons goes directly toward keeping the platform free, private, and independent. No investors. No algorithms optimised for engagement. No hidden agenda.

  • 🏡  Hosting & infrastructure costs covered by the community
  • 🔒  Zero advertising — ever
  • 📜  Open governance — decisions made with the people, not above them
  • 🌍  Future: multilingual support so everyone can participate

Donate — Power to the People

The Commons is not a registered charity. Donations are voluntary contributions toward platform infrastructure and development. No goods or services are provided in exchange.

Because The Commons is not a registered charity, donations are not tax deductible. That makes every contribution an act of pure generosity — and we are deeply grateful for it.
